My research sits at the intersection of mathematical biology, epidemiology, and data science. I use mechanistic models and statistical methods to understand how infectious diseases spread, how human behavior shapes epidemic outcomes, and how populations respond to environmental challenges.

### Current Projects

---

#### [How Movement Decisions Shape Disease Spread](/research/behavioral-epidemiology/)

As the COVID-19 pandemic demonstrated, people change how often they travel and where they go during disease outbreaks — but their perception of risk doesn't always match how the disease actually spreads. We show that risk-avoidant changes in travel can sometimes *increase* transmission if there is a mismatch between perceived and actual risk. 

**[Explore the interactive results →](/research/simple-trips/)**

---

#### [Tasmanian Devils and Transmissible Cancer: Coevolution and Persistence](/research/tasmanian-devils/)


Tasmanian devils are at risk of extinction from a fatal, transmissible cancer. Using mechanistic modeling, we show that rapid evolution could play a major role in their continued survival.

**[Explore the interactive results →](/research/tasmanian-devils/)**, or read our **[full paper](<https://doi.org/10.1093/evolut/qpae143>)** published in *Evolution*.

---

#### [Spatiotemporal Variation in SARS-CoV-2 Transmission](/research/sars-cov2-bayesian/)

Using Bayesian hierarchical models and case data from 10 U.S. states, we are estimating how and why SARS-CoV-2 transmission rates varied across geography and time — improving the statistical foundations for future epidemic forecasting.

---

#### [Mosquito and West Nile Virus Distribution in Forsyth County](/research/mosquito-west-nile/)

In collaboration with the Forsyth County Department of Public Health, we are mapping when and where mosquito populations and West Nile Virus activity are highest in the county to support targeted public health interventions.

#### Prior Research

During my PhD at UC Davis, I developed population models to understand how organisms evolve in response to rapid environmental change — and whether that evolution can prevent extinction. I also studied how environmental variability shapes food-caching behavior in rodents and the ecology of plant-animal seed dispersal. See my [Publications](/Publications/) for details.
