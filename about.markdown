I am a quantitative biologist and epidemiologist with over eight years of experience in statistical modeling, data analysis, and scientific writing. I began my career as an ecologist and evolutionary biologist before shifting my focus to disease modeling — first in natural populations and then in humans. I have led multiple research projects in collaboration with interdisciplinary teams, consistently maintaining high standards of scientific rigor and reproducibility.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the [Kortessis Lab](https://nickkortessis.weebly.com) in the Department of Biology at Wake Forest University, where I develop epidemiological models that incorporate human behavior and movement. My broad background spans human mobility, the ecology of vector-borne pathogens, and the evolution of disease resistance.
